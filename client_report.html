Client Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I felt that when making a website it should be information that could be easily intuited by those making the website for me. A part of the decision for the images was the decision to go along with a certain theme for the website.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

We communicated through Microsoft Teams. I tried to respond to every question I received from the team within two hours of the message being sent. I also would occasionally just check in on the team every few days to make sure there were no resources that I needed to provide for them.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

In a way yes and in a way no. There were individuals who participated in the project who were very engaged through the process. I was very happy with the people who did this and they helped the website come to life. On the other hand, those who were less engaged did not do their part of the work for the project. This then led to the website not taking on a life in those sections.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I think I would like to set expectations a lot more clearly early on in the project, especially for those junior developers. That way we would be able to get through any hiccups more smoothly.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

This was through Microsoft Teams. I wanted each junior developer to know which child page they had to do. I tried to communicate when my portion of the project would be finished so they could complete their part. One of the junior devs has not been very communicative, but that is ok.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

There were a few issues in communicating with the client. They did not communicate with us for the first half of the project. This would have been usable, but the client did not include key information that was needed about the website. We needed to make progress on the project, but since the client was not communicating, we had to make some decisions about the website's wireframe. I wish we could have gone along with what the client wanted, but there simply was not enough information in a timely manner.

How did you handle publishing your client's site and sharing it with the client?

I published the site on GitHub and shared the link to the site on Microsoft Teams.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

At first it was very frustrating, and there are still some issues I have with it, but it has become very easy to grab a link and to coordinate our website changes.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

It was through Microsoft Teams. There were, again, a couple issues with some team leads disappearing for the majority of the process.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

For one site I knew what was expected of me. I was expected to edit the Skyrim child page. The other site I did not hear from the head developer about the project until 3.5 weeks into the 4-week project. This was frustrating because the project was wrapping up already when the lead developer was able to finish his portion of the work.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

I was able to contribute to the website for the Skyrim/Bethesda website. The work on this website was completed in a timely manner. The other website was only completed days before the project wrap up and it was in the middle of finals so there was little chance to contribute.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

Not particularly. There was a small hiccup where I accidentally rewrote some code, but I was able to find a way to undo this.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

Something that was challenging through the process was when someone was not able to do the work they were allotted. When I wanted to work on something, but a teammate was not being forthcoming about their work it either slowed down or entirely stopped the work happening. I think I overcame it with some groups where I was very direct about the results that I could produce in the allotted time. Other parts of the project went unnoticed for some time. This then led to the impression by one person that, even though he had finally completed week 2 of the project despite it being in the middle of week 4, we would be able to support him despite the project almost being over now.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed collaboration when there was teamwork. I liked staying up to date on the different projects and I liked the sense of responsibility that I had for the people that were following me along the way.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would be more aware of the different choices made on the sites I was working on. I sometimes did not check on links when they were shared with me. This hurt my ability to help in the creation process.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes, I feel more confident in my ability to communicate project needs and what I, as an individual, can deliver as an employee. I have seen parts of this process in other work areas, but I have never been on one myself in a workplace context. This delivered all the frustration and hope that such a project, I think, should bring minus the high stakes.
